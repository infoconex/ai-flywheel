# AI Flywheel History and Provenance

The AI Flywheel methodology was not created at the moment its formal documentation was written. Its underlying ideas developed through practical experimentation and iterative refinement before they were organized into the current named specification.

This page distinguishes **concept development**, **formalization**, and **public release** so the history of the methodology is clear without implying that unpublished work was publicly available earlier than it was.

## Timeline

### January 2026 — Concept Development Begins

The ideas that later became the AI Flywheel began taking shape through practical work with AI that could create code and other capabilities for its own operational use, follow persistent procedures, handle execution failures, preserve useful lessons, and improve future execution.

The initial focus was practical rather than terminological: how to move from using AI to create tools for a human operator toward AI operating, evaluating, and improving the system by which the work itself is performed.

### January–June 2026 — Iterative Refinement

The approach continued to evolve through experimentation and repeated use.

During this period, the developing model increasingly distinguished among concepts that would later become explicit parts of the methodology, including:

- AI as the operator of the process rather than only an assistant,
- deterministic capability, procedural guidance, and AI reasoning as different operating mechanisms,
- execution evidence as the basis for evaluating outcomes,
- failure and learning as signals for deciding where the system should evolve,
- persistent operational learning that survives an individual execution,
- reuse as the mechanism that creates compounding improvement,
- and human authority as the boundary around autonomous operation and self-improvement.

The terminology and formal structure used by the current specification were not necessarily fixed during this period. The methodology emerged through refinement of the underlying operating ideas.

### July 2026 — Formalization as the AI Flywheel

In July 2026, the accumulated ideas were organized into the named **AI Flywheel** methodology and developed into a coherent documentation set.

The formalized model introduced a canonical definition, eight named principles, an eight-stage lifecycle, an explicit governance and boundary model, a conformance assessment, architecture views, worked examples, and supporting prior-art research.

This period marks the formal organization and documentation of the methodology, not the beginning of the underlying ideas.

### July 2026 — Specification 0.1 Draft Prepared for Public Release

The first formal specification was prepared as **AI Flywheel Specification 0.1 — Draft**, with the first repository release planned as `v0.1.0`.

The 0.1 designation reflects that the methodology is being made available as a coherent working specification while remaining open to refinement through implementation experience, public review, and continued research.

## Interpreting These Dates

The dates on this page represent different milestones:

- **January 2026 — Origin of concept development:** the underlying ideas began being developed and refined.
- **July 2026 — Formalization:** the ideas were organized into the named AI Flywheel methodology and specification structure.
- **First `v0.1.0` publication — First formal public release:** the first intentionally released version of the documented methodology.
