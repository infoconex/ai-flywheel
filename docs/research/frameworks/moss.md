## Working Hypothesis

MOSS is expected to provide strong prior art for using execution evidence to drive persistent source-level improvements.

The main distinctions to investigate are whether adaptation has one dominant destination, source modification, or multiple persistent destinations, and how human authority constrains changes to the agent substrate.

## Evidence to Collect

- The adaptation pipeline.
- The role of production failures or execution traces.
- Candidate generation and validation mechanisms.
- Rollback and safety mechanisms.
- Whether procedures and runtime judgment are independent adaptation targets.
- Human approval or governance requirements for persistent changes.
- Whether the system can expand its own authority or only modify capabilities within a fixed authority boundary.

## Provisional Position

Do not claim evidence-driven self-modification as unique to the AI Flywheel. Focus comparison on multi-destination evolution routing, responsibility allocation, and human-governed authority.
