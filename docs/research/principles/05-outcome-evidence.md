# Principle 5 Research: Outcome Evidence

## Principle Under Review

Execution must produce enough evidence to determine what happened, whether the intended result occurred, and where assumptions or behavior failed.

See the canonical [principles specification](../../specification/principles.md#principle-5-execution-must-produce-outcome-evidence).

## Prior-Art Question

How do related systems capture and evaluate real execution outcomes, feedback signals, trajectories, tests, failures, human decisions, or external observations?

## Working Assessment

Evidence-driven improvement is clearly expected to be established prior art. The research should focus on the nature and role of evidence rather than claiming outcome observation as novel.

## Comparison Dimensions

- What evidence is captured?
- Is success externally validated or inferred by the model?
- Are failures retained for future analysis?
- Are human judgments and approvals retained as evidence?
- Does evidence change persistent operational assets?
- Is evidence used to decide where an improvement belongs?
- Is evidence used to determine whether future escalation can be reduced safely?
