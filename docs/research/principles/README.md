# Principle Research Dossiers

This section does **not** define the AI Flywheel principles. The canonical principles are maintained in the [AI Flywheel specification](../../specification/principles/README.md).

These dossiers investigate the ideas behind each principle. Their purpose is to record:

- related concepts and prior art,
- historical provenance and establishment dates that still need to be verified,
- evidence gathered from related frameworks,
- similarities and differences that may affect differentiation claims,
- unanswered questions,
- evidence that still needs to be collected,
- and the current research position based on what is known so far.

A research dossier may remain incomplete or change as new evidence is found. The specification should remain the authoritative statement of what the AI Flywheel is.

## Research Status Language

The dossiers use four broad states:

- **Established:** supported by clear primary-source evidence.
- **Current evidence:** supported by the framework and source review completed so far.
- **Open:** a research question that has not been answered sufficiently.
- **Not established:** the reviewed sources do not currently demonstrate the mechanism; this does not prove that no implementation exists.

For framework-by-framework evidence, see the [framework and prior-art research section](../frameworks/README.md) and [framework comparison matrix](../frameworks/framework-comparison-matrix.md).
