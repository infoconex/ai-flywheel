# Principle 7 Research: Persistent Operational Learning

## Principle Under Review

Useful learning must survive the current execution by changing a durable operational asset.

See the canonical [principles specification](../../specification/principles.md#principle-7-learning-must-change-a-persistent-operational-asset).

## Prior-Art Question

How do existing frameworks persist lessons as memory, skills, procedures, tools, source changes, governance changes, or other reusable artifacts?

## Working Assessment

Persistent learning is expected to have strong prior art across several frameworks. This principle should be treated primarily as a required property of the AI Flywheel rather than an independent novelty claim.

## Comparison Dimensions

- What artifact persists?
- Is the artifact executable, procedural, textual, architectural, or governance-related?
- How is it validated before reuse?
- Is authorization required before persistence?
- Is it scoped to one task, one environment, or many tasks?
- Can one type of persistent asset later be converted into another?
- Can human decisions become durable operational guidance?
